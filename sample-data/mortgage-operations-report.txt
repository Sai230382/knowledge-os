EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
─────────────────
Pacific Coast Mortgage Group processed 4,287 loan applications in Q4 2025,
representing $1.42B in total loan volume. Net funded volume was $1.18B across
3,614 closed loans. The average loan size increased 8.3% to $326,500, driven
primarily by jumbo and non-QM product growth. Our pull-through rate improved
from 72% to 84.3% following the implementation of the automated pre-qualification
engine developed by the technology team under CTO Raj Mehta.

The company operates across 14 branch offices in California, Oregon, Washington,
and Arizona. Our wholesale channel contributed 38% of total volume while retail
represented 62%. The correspondent lending division, launched in Q3 under
VP Sarah Chen, is still in pilot phase with $42M in volume.

SECTION 2: LOAN ORIGINATION PROCESS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STEP 1 — LEAD CAPTURE & PRE-QUALIFICATION
  Sources: Direct web (32%), Zillow/LendingTree leads (24%), Realtor
  referrals (28%), Past client referrals (16%)

  The LoanFlow CRM automatically scores incoming leads using a proprietary
  algorithm that factors in credit tier estimates, property location, and
  loan amount. High-priority leads are routed to senior LOs within 5 minutes.

  Pre-qualification uses soft credit pull via CreditXpert and preliminary
  AUS findings via Desktop Underwriter (DU).

STEP 2 — APPLICATION & DISCLOSURE
  Full 1003 application completed via LoanFlow borrower portal or in-person.
  System auto-generates initial disclosures (LE, TILA, etc.) within
  3 business days per TRID requirements.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: Processor Maria Santos has discovered that if the LE is
  regenerated after a program change within the first 3 days, it resets
  the TRID clock. This is not documented anywhere but has been confirmed
  by compliance. Multiple processors now rely on this workaround.

STEP 3 — PROCESSING
  Average processing time: 4.2 days (down from 6.8 days in Q3)
  Processors order: appraisal, title, HOI, VOE, VOD, tax transcripts

  Automated verification via The Work Number (Equifax) covers 61% of
  employment verifications. Remaining 39% require manual VOE, adding
  2-3 days to processing time.

  EXCEPTION: Self-employed borrowers with S-Corp structures and officer
  compensation below market rates trigger additional review. Underwriter
  Lisa Chang developed an informal "reasonableness test" comparing officer
  comp to industry benchmarks via BLS data — this isn't in the official
  guidelines but catches 80% of income misrepresentation cases.

STEP 4 — UNDERWRITING
  Three-tier review system:
    Tier 1 — AUS-approved, standard docs → Junior underwriter (auto-assign)
    Tier 2 — AUS-approved with conditions → Senior underwriter
    Tier 3 — Manual underwrite, non-QM, exceptions → VP review

  Average underwriting decision time:
    Tier 1: 1.2 days | Tier 2: 2.4 days | Tier 3: 4.7 days

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: For VA loans with multiple periods of service, the
  underwriting team has learned that the VA Regional Loan Center in
  Phoenix processes COE requests 2-3 days faster than other centers.
  Thomas Rodriguez routes all complex VA eligibility cases through Phoenix
  even when the borrower's state would normally route elsewhere.

STEP 5 — CONDITIONAL APPROVAL & CLEARING CONDITIONS
  Average conditions per file: 6.3 (Tier 1), 12.8 (Tier 2), 22.1 (Tier 3)
  Average days to clear conditions: 5.4 days
  Most common conditions: Updated paystubs (78%), Explanation letters (65%),
  Gift letter documentation (34%), Source & season large deposits (31%)

STEP 6 — CLEAR TO CLOSE & CLOSING
  Closing department managed by Director Karen Wu. Team of 6 closers.
  Average time from CTC to funding: 3.2 days
  E-closing adoption rate: 42% (hybrid) and 8% (full RON)

  EXCEPTION: California-specific notary requirements for deed of trust
  with power of sale clause require wet signatures for the deed even in
  hybrid e-closing states. Karen Wu's team has a workaround: they schedule
  a mobile notary for just the deed while everything else is e-signed.
  This saves 1-2 days vs. full wet closing.

STEP 7 — POST-CLOSING & SECONDARY MARKETING
  Loans packaged for sale within 15 days of funding. James Park's team
  manages a $280M pipeline hedge using TBA forward sales and options.

  Investor suspense rate: 4.2% (industry avg: 8.1%)
  Top suspense reasons: Missing HMDA data (28%), Title policy delays (24%),
  Trailing documents (19%), Appraisal reconsideration of value (14%)

  - Encompass API rate limits cause batch processing failures during

    occur ~3% of the time, requiring manual lock desk intervention.
    James Park's team has built a reconciliation script that catches
    most of these before loan lock.

SECTION 5: COMPLIANCE & REGULATORY LANDSCAPE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CURRENT REGULATORY FOCUS AREAS:

1. TRID (TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure)
   David Kim's team audits 15% of all files for TRID compliance.
   Q4 violation rate: 1.8% (down from 3.2% in Q3)
   Most common violations: Changed circumstance documentation (44%),
   Fee tolerance cures (31%), Timing of revised LE (25%)

   TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: Compliance analyst Rachel Torres discovered that
   Encompass auto-calculates tolerance cures incorrectly when there are
   multiple changed circumstances on the same fee line item. She created
   a manual spreadsheet tracker that the compliance team uses to verify
   all tolerance cure calculations. This has prevented 12 potential
   regulatory violations in Q4.

2. ECOA / Fair Lending
   HMDA data analyzed quarterly for potential disparities.
   AI-assisted fair lending analysis implemented via Compliance Sentinel.
   No material findings in Q4. However, denial rate disparity between
   Hispanic and non-Hispanic white applicants in the Sacramento MSA
   requires monitoring (8.2 percentage point gap, partially explained
   by credit score distribution but 2.1 points remain unexplained).

3. State-Specific Licensing
   Operating in 4 states with varying requirements:
   - California (DRE & DFPI dual licensing)
   - Oregon (DCBS licensing, unique fee disclosure rules)
   - Washington (DFI licensing, cooling-off period requirements)
   - Arizona (DIFI licensing, standard requirements)

   EXCEPTION: Oregon's unique "Total Loan Cost" disclosure must be
   provided in addition to the federal LE. The LoanFlow system generates
   this automatically, but when loans transfer between branches across
   state lines, the Oregon disclosure sometimes doesn't generate.
   Patricia Huang implemented a manual checklist for inter-branch
   transfers but it's still missed about 5% of the time.

4. Flood Insurance Compliance
   FEMA map changes in Q4 affected 127 properties in our pipeline.
   23 loans required flood insurance that wasn't initially anticipated.
   The appraisal team now checks FEMA flood zone status at ordering
   (not just at closing) — a process change implemented after two loans
   had to be delayed 2 weeks for emergency flood insurance procurement.

SECTION 6: RISK MANAGEMENT & QUALITY CONTROL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PRE-FUNDING QC:
  - 100% of government loans reviewed pre-funding
  - 20% random sample of conventional loans
  - All non-QM and jumbo loans reviewed by senior underwriter
  - Defect rate: 2.8% (industry benchmark: 3.5-5%)

POST-CLOSING QC:
  - 10% random sample across all channels
  - Performed by third-party QC firm (Aces Quality Management)
  - Critical defect rate: 0.4% (industry benchmark: 1.2%)
  - Most common findings: Missing signatures on non-critical docs (38%),
    Income calculation rounding differences (22%), Missing HOA cert (18%)

FRAUD DETECTION:
  - LoanGuard AI fraud detection integrated with Encompass
  - Flagged 89 files in Q4 for potential issues
  - 12 confirmed fraud attempts intercepted (occupancy fraud: 7,
    income fraud: 3, identity fraud: 2)
  - Estimated loss prevention: $3.2M

REPURCHASE RISK:
  - Total outstanding repurchase requests: 8 loans ($2.8M UPB)
  - Successfully defended 5 requests via Aces audit trails
  - Settled 2 requests at 40% of original loss claim
  - 1 pending review
