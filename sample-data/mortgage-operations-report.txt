              MORTGAGE OPERATIONS — END-TO-END VALUE CHAIN
              Process, Technology & Operational Knowledge Base
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

OVERVIEW
─────────
This document describes the full mortgage lifecycle from loan origination
through servicing and post-closing. It covers the key roles involved,
technology systems used, process workflows, and operational knowledge
that teams have accumulated — including undocumented workarounds and
exception-handling practices.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. LOAN ORIGINATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

LEAD CAPTURE & PRE-QUALIFICATION
  Lead sources typically include direct web inquiries, aggregator leads
  (Zillow, LendingTree), realtor referral networks, and past borrower
  referrals. Leads are scored in the CRM based on estimated credit tier,
  property location, and requested loan amount. High-priority leads are
  routed to Senior Loan Officers within minutes.

  Pre-qualification involves a soft credit pull through CreditXpert and
  a preliminary AUS (Automated Underwriting System) run through Desktop
  Underwriter (DU) to give the borrower an early eligibility signal.

  ROLES INVOLVED: Loan Officer, Loan Officer Assistant, Branch Manager

APPLICATION & INITIAL DISCLOSURES
  The borrower completes a full 1003 Uniform Residential Loan Application
  either through the lender's borrower portal or in person. The system
  auto-generates the initial Loan Estimate (LE) and required TILA
  disclosures within 3 business days per TRID regulations.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: If the Loan Estimate needs to be regenerated after
  a program change within the first 3 business days, it resets the TRID
  disclosure clock. This is not documented in most compliance manuals
  but has been confirmed through regulatory counsel. Processing teams
  rely on this workaround to buy time when borrowers change loan programs
  early in the process.

  TECHNOLOGY: Encompass LOS (ICE Mortgage Technology) handles the 1003,
  fee management, and disclosure generation. The borrower portal is
  typically a proprietary or vendor-supplied system integrated via API.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. PROCESSING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Once the application is submitted, the Loan Processor takes over. Their
job is to assemble a complete loan file for underwriting review.

TASKS PERFORMED BY PROCESSOR:
  - Order appraisal through an Appraisal Management Company (AMC)
  - Order title search and title insurance commitment
  - Verify employment (VOE) — automated via The Work Number (Equifax)
    for W-2 borrowers; manual verification required for self-employed
  - Verify assets/deposits (VOD) via bank statement review or asset
    verification services like Plaid or Finicity
  - Order tax transcripts (IRS 4506-C) to validate reported income
  - Order homeowner's insurance (HOI) binder
  - Collect any missing borrower documentation

AUTOMATED vs. MANUAL VERIFICATION:
  The Work Number covers roughly 60% of employment verifications
  automatically. The remaining 40% — especially gig workers, 1099
  contractors, and small business employees — require manual VOE,
  which adds 2-3 days to processing time.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: Asset verification through Plaid sometimes fails
  for credit unions and smaller banks. When this happens, experienced
  processors know to request 60-day bank statements directly rather
  than waiting for a Plaid retry, which rarely resolves the connection
  issue and just delays the file.

  EXCEPTION: Self-employed borrowers with S-Corp structures where
  officer compensation is below market rates trigger additional review.
  Experienced underwriters apply an informal "reasonableness test"
  comparing officer comp to Bureau of Labor Statistics data. This
  is not in the official underwriting guidelines but catches income
  misrepresentation early before the file goes to formal underwriting.

ROLES INVOLVED: Loan Processor, Processor Manager, Appraisal Desk


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2B. PROPERTY VALUATION (APPRAISAL)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Property valuation is a critical step that runs in parallel with
processing. The appraisal determines the property's market value, which
directly affects the loan-to-value (LTV) ratio and loan eligibility.

APPRAISAL ORDERING:
  Appraisals must be ordered through an Appraisal Management Company
  (AMC) to maintain independence per Dodd-Frank requirements. The lender
  cannot directly select or influence the appraiser. Common AMCs include
  CoreLogic, Clarocity, ServiceLink, and Solidifi.

  Typical appraisal turnaround: 5-10 business days (varies by market)
  Rural properties and unique homes can take 2-3 weeks due to limited
  appraiser availability and difficulty finding comparable sales.

APPRAISAL TYPES:
  Full appraisal (1004/70): Interior and exterior inspection with
    comparable sales analysis — required for most purchase transactions
  Exterior-only (2055): Drive-by inspection — used for some refinances
  Desktop appraisal: No physical inspection, uses MLS data and public
    records — allowed by GSEs for certain low-risk refinances
  Appraisal waiver: AUS issues a Property Inspection Waiver (PIW) —
    no appraisal needed, based on data confidence in the AUS model

APPRAISAL REVIEW:
  The Appraisal Desk or a Staff Review Appraiser reviews every completed
  appraisal for:
    - Comparable sales selection (appropriate proximity, recency, similarity)
    - Adjustments reasonableness (line adjustments under 10%, net under 15%)
    - Subject property condition and marketability
    - Compliance with USPAP (Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice)

  EXCEPTION: When the appraised value comes in below the purchase price
  ("short appraisal"), the loan team has limited options:
    1. Request a Reconsideration of Value (ROV) with additional comps
    2. Buyer renegotiates purchase price with seller
    3. Buyer brings additional cash to cover the gap
    4. Switch to a different loan product with higher LTV tolerance
  The ROV process is delicate — the appraiser is not obligated to change
  their opinion. Providing strong comparable sales that the appraiser
  may have missed is the most effective approach.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: When requesting a Reconsideration of Value, experienced
  Loan Officers know to provide comps that closed AFTER the appraiser's
  effective date, not before — appraisers are more receptive to new data
  they couldn't have had at the time of the original report. Also, sending
  the ROV through the AMC (not directly to the appraiser) is required by
  regulation, but some AMCs have a 48-hour internal processing delay.
  Teams that have identified which AMCs respond fastest route time-sensitive
  ROVs to those AMCs when possible.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: For FHA appraisals, any health and safety issues
  flagged by the appraiser (peeling paint, broken handrails, exposed
  wiring) must be repaired BEFORE closing — unlike conventional loans
  where these can be addressed post-closing. Experienced processors
  immediately flag FHA files with property condition issues to the
  Loan Officer so repair negotiations start early, avoiding last-minute
  closing delays.

TECHNOLOGY:
  Mercury Network / Reggora — AMC order management platforms
  CoreLogic AVM (Automated Valuation Model) — used for desktop appraisals
  MLS data feeds — comparable sales data

ROLES INVOLVED: Appraiser (independent, via AMC), Staff Review Appraiser,
  Appraisal Desk Coordinator, AMC Account Manager


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. UNDERWRITING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Underwriting is where credit risk is assessed and the loan is either
approved, suspended, or denied.

THREE-TIER REVIEW SYSTEM:
  Tier 1 — AUS-approved, standard documentation, clean credit
           Assigned to Junior Underwriter, expected turnaround: 1 day
  Tier 2 — AUS-approved but with conditions (overlays, exceptions)
           Assigned to Senior Underwriter, expected turnaround: 2-3 days
  Tier 3 — Manual underwrite, non-QM products, complex scenarios
           Requires VP of Underwriting review, turnaround: 4-5 days

AUS ENGINES:
  Desktop Underwriter (DU) by Fannie Mae — primary for conventional loans
  Loan Product Advisor (LPA) by Freddie Mac — secondary, used for
    comparison runs and Freddie-specific products
  GUS (Government Underwriting System) — used for USDA loans
  FHA TOTAL Scorecard — for FHA mortgage insurance approval

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: For VA loans with multiple periods of service, the
  VA Regional Loan Center in Phoenix processes Certificate of Eligibility
  (COE) requests 2-3 days faster than other centers. Seasoned underwriters
  route all complex VA eligibility cases through Phoenix regardless of
  borrower's state, saving significant time on these files.

CONDITIONAL APPROVAL:
  Most loans receive a conditional approval with a list of "conditions"
  (stipulations) that must be satisfied before final approval.
  Average conditions per file:
    Tier 1: 6 conditions | Tier 2: 13 conditions | Tier 3: 22 conditions
  Common conditions: updated paystubs, explanation letters for credit
  inquiries, gift letter documentation, large deposit sourcing.

  EXCEPTION: When a borrower's debt-to-income ratio is borderline
  (45-49.99% on conventional), some AUS systems approve with a finding
  that is not a true DU Approve but rather an "Eligible" with risk
  factors. Many investors will not purchase these files. Underwriters
  maintain an informal "investor tolerance matrix" that maps which
  investors accept which edge-case AUS findings — this is not published
  anywhere and is maintained through experience and relationship knowledge.

ROLES INVOLVED: Junior Underwriter, Senior Underwriter, VP of Underwriting


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. CLEAR TO CLOSE & CLOSING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Once all conditions are cleared, the underwriter issues a "Clear to Close"
(CTC). The file moves to the Closing Department.

CLOSING PROCESS:
  - Final Closing Disclosure (CD) prepared and sent to borrower
  - 3-day waiting period per TRID before closing can occur
  - Title company or settlement agent coordinates the closing
  - Closing can be wet-sign (in person), hybrid e-close, or full RON
    (Remote Online Notarization)

E-CLOSING ADOPTION:
  Hybrid e-closing (most docs e-signed, deed/note wet-signed): ~42%
  Full RON (fully remote): ~8% — limited to states that allow it
  Traditional wet-sign: ~50%

  EXCEPTION: In California, deed of trust documents with power of sale
  clause require wet notarized signatures even in hybrid e-closing
  scenarios. Closing teams work around this by scheduling a mobile
  notary specifically for the deed while everything else is e-signed.
  This saves 1-2 days vs. requiring a full wet closing.

FUNDING:
  Wire instructions sent to title company on closing day. Lender funds
  the loan after receiving the signed closing package and confirming
  all conditions are met. Average time from CTC to funding: 3 days.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: Some title companies in Oregon have a 24-hour
  recording delay that other states don't have. If the closer doesn't
  account for this, the funding date shifts, which can violate lock
  expiration deadlines. Experienced closers always add 1 buffer day
  for Oregon closings in their scheduling.

TECHNOLOGY:
  DocuSign — used for e-signing disclosures and non-notarized docs
  Notarize — RON platform for fully remote closings
  Encompass — generates closing docs, CD, and final compliance check

ROLES INVOLVED: Closer, Closing Manager, Title Company, Settlement Agent


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. POST-CLOSING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

After funding, the loan enters post-closing operations.

DOCUMENT RECORDING:
  The closing package (deed of trust, mortgage note) is sent to the
  county recorder's office. Recording timelines vary by county — some
  are fully electronic (same day), others are paper-based (2-6 weeks).

TRAILING DOCUMENTS:
  Any documents not received at closing (final title policy, recorded
  deed, HOA certifications) must be collected within 60-90 days.
  Post-closing teams track outstanding trailing docs and follow up
  with title companies and borrowers.

INVESTOR DELIVERY & LOAN SALE:
  Loans are packaged and delivered to investors (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac,
  Ginnie Mae, or private investors) typically within 15 days of funding.

  SUSPENSE: Investors may "suspend" a delivered loan if documentation
  is missing or data doesn't match. Common suspense reasons:
    - Missing or incorrect HMDA data (28%)
    - Title policy delays (24%)
    - Trailing documents not yet received (19%)
    - Appraisal reconsideration of value pending (14%)
    - Data discrepancies between LOS and delivery file (15%)

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: When delivering to Fannie Mae via their portal,
  the HMDA data fields must match exactly between Encompass and the
  delivery file. A common mismatch occurs on the "rate spread" field
  when the loan was repriced after lock. Experienced secondary marketing
  analysts always manually verify rate spread on repriced loans before
  delivery — the LOS doesn't auto-update this field reliably.

ROLES INVOLVED: Post-Closer, Shipping Clerk, Secondary Marketing Analyst


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. LOAN SERVICING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Some lenders retain servicing rights, others sell them. For retained
servicing:

SERVICING ACTIVITIES:
  - Payment collection and processing
  - Escrow management (property taxes, insurance)
  - Investor remittance (monthly principal & interest pass-through)
  - Borrower communications (statements, tax forms 1098)
  - Loss mitigation if borrower becomes delinquent

ESCROW MANAGEMENT:
  Annual escrow analysis required to ensure borrower's monthly escrow
  payment covers projected tax and insurance disbursements. If there's
  a shortage, the servicer must notify the borrower and offer a payment
  adjustment or lump-sum option.

  EXCEPTION: In some counties, property tax reassessments happen mid-year
  (supplemental tax bills). The escrow analysis system doesn't always
  pick these up automatically. Servicing teams in California have learned
  to manually check for supplemental tax bills within 6 months of any
  purchase transaction — if missed, the escrow account can go into
  significant shortage, causing borrower complaints and potential
  regulatory issues.

SERVICING TRANSFER:
  When servicing rights are sold, a "goodbye/hello" letter process is
  triggered. The outgoing servicer sends a goodbye letter 15 days before
  transfer; the incoming servicer sends a hello letter. Payment processing
  must continue without interruption during the 60-day transfer window.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: During servicing transfers, autopay enrollments
  do NOT automatically transfer. Borrowers who had autopay set up with
  the old servicer often miss their first payment with the new servicer
  because they assume it carries over. Experienced servicing teams
  proactively call autopay borrowers during the transfer window to
  prevent delinquency.

TECHNOLOGY:
  Black Knight MSP or Sagent LoanServ — primary servicing platforms
  CoreLogic Tax Services — property tax monitoring
  Escrow analysis engines built into servicing platform

ROLES INVOLVED: Servicing Specialist, Escrow Analyst, Loss Mitigation
  Specialist, Servicing Manager


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7. COMPLIANCE & REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Mortgage lending is one of the most heavily regulated industries. Key
regulations that affect every stage of the value chain:

TRID (TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure):
  Governs timing and accuracy of Loan Estimate and Closing Disclosure.
  Common violations: changed circumstance documentation gaps, fee
  tolerance cure miscalculations, timing of revised Loan Estimates.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: Encompass auto-calculates tolerance cures incorrectly
  when there are multiple changed circumstances on the same fee line item.
  Compliance teams who have discovered this use a manual spreadsheet
  tracker to verify all tolerance cure calculations before closing.
  This workaround has prevented numerous potential regulatory violations.

ECOA (Equal Credit Opportunity Act) / FAIR LENDING:
  HMDA data must be collected and reported annually. Lenders analyze
  denial rates across protected classes (race, ethnicity, gender, age)
  to identify potential disparate impact. AI-assisted fair lending
  analysis tools are increasingly used to detect subtle patterns.

RESPA (Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act):
  Governs fee disclosures, prohibits kickbacks and referral fees.
  Affiliated business arrangements must be disclosed.

STATE-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
  Each state has unique licensing and disclosure requirements.
  Example: Oregon requires a "Total Loan Cost" disclosure in addition
  to the federal Loan Estimate. When loans transfer between branches
  across state lines, state-specific disclosures sometimes don't
  auto-generate in the LOS — requiring manual intervention.

  EXCEPTION: When a loan closes in one state but the property is in
  another (common in border areas like Portland/Vancouver), both states'
  disclosure requirements must be met. This dual-state scenario is not
  well-handled by most LOS systems and requires manual compliance review.

ROLES INVOLVED: Compliance Officer, Compliance Analyst, HMDA Reporting
  Specialist, Quality Control Reviewer


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
8. QUALITY CONTROL & RISK MANAGEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PRE-FUNDING QC:
  - 100% of government loans (FHA, VA, USDA) reviewed before funding
  - Random sample (15-20%) of conventional loans
  - All non-QM and jumbo loans reviewed by a senior underwriter
  Typical pre-funding defect rate: 2-3%

POST-CLOSING QC:
  - 10% random sample across all channels and products
  - Often performed by a third-party QC firm (e.g., Aces Quality
    Management, JMAC, or Digital Risk)
  - Common findings: missing signatures on non-critical docs, income
    calculation rounding differences, missing HOA certifications

FRAUD DETECTION:
  AI-powered fraud detection tools (e.g., LoanGuard, PointPredictive)
  are integrated with the LOS to flag suspicious patterns:
    - Occupancy fraud (borrower claims primary residence but isn't)
    - Income fraud (inflated income or fabricated employment)
    - Identity fraud (synthetic identity or stolen identity)
    - Property flipping schemes

REPURCHASE RISK:
  If an investor discovers a defect after purchasing the loan, they
  can demand the lender repurchase it. Maintaining clean audit trails
  and thorough QC reduces repurchase exposure significantly.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: When responding to investor repurchase demands,
  experienced secondary marketing teams know that providing a detailed
  remediation plan (instead of just disputing the finding) leads to
  a settlement at 30-50% of the original loss claim in most cases.
  Flat denials almost always escalate to full repurchase demands.

ROLES INVOLVED: QC Reviewer, QC Manager, Fraud Analyst, Risk Manager


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9. TECHNOLOGY ECOSYSTEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The mortgage technology stack typically includes:

LOAN ORIGINATION SYSTEM (LOS):
  Encompass (ICE Mortgage Technology) — market leader
  Handles: loan application, fee management, disclosure generation,
  document management, workflow automation, compliance checks

PRICING ENGINE:
  Optimal Blue — real-time pricing across multiple investors
  Handles: rate locks, lock extensions, renegotiations, best-execution
  analysis across investor/product combinations

CRM & LEAD MANAGEMENT:
  Velocify, Salesforce, or proprietary systems
  Handles: lead routing, follow-up automation, pipeline management

AUTOMATED UNDERWRITING:
  Desktop Underwriter (DU) — Fannie Mae
  Loan Product Advisor (LPA) — Freddie Mac
  FHA TOTAL Scorecard — HUD/FHA
  GUS — USDA

VERIFICATION SERVICES:
  The Work Number (Equifax) — employment verification
  Plaid / Finicity — asset verification
  CreditXpert — credit scoring and simulation

E-CLOSING:
  DocuSign — electronic signatures
  Notarize / Snapdocs — remote online notarization (RON)

COMPLIANCE:
  ComplianceEase / Compliance Sentinel — regulatory monitoring
  HMDA reporting tools integrated with LOS

SERVICING PLATFORMS:
  Black Knight MSP — dominant servicing platform
  Sagent LoanServ — alternative servicing system
  CoreLogic — property tax monitoring and flood zone verification

INTEGRATION CHALLENGES:
  - LOS API rate limits during peak hours cause batch processing delays
  - Employment verification data is stale for gig economy workers
  - Pricing engine discrepancies with investor rate sheets occur ~3%
    of the time, requiring manual lock desk intervention
  - Different investor delivery portals have different data format
    requirements, making multi-investor delivery error-prone


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
10. NOTICE OF COMPLETION (NOC) & DOCUMENT RETENTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

After all post-closing activities are complete — loan is delivered to
the investor, all trailing docs received, recording confirmed — the
file receives a Notice of Completion status.

DOCUMENT RETENTION:
  Federal and state regulations require mortgage documents to be retained
  for specific periods:
    - 3 years for TRID-related disclosures
    - 5 years for HMDA data
    - 7 years for general loan documents
    - Permanent retention for recorded instruments

  Most lenders use document management systems (e.g., FileNet, Docutech)
  integrated with their LOS for long-term document storage.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: During regulatory examinations, examiners often
  request documents organized in a specific order that doesn't match how
  the LOS stores them. Compliance teams who have been through multiple
  exams maintain a "regulatory exam prep template" that reorganizes the
  loan file into the examiner's expected format. This undocumented
  template saves days of preparation time during audits.

ROLES INVOLVED: Post-Closing Manager, Document Custodian, Compliance
  Officer, Records Management
