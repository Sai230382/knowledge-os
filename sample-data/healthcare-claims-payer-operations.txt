EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
─────────────────
Meridian Health Partners is a regional health plan serving 1.2 million
members across Commercial (HMO/PPO), Medicare Advantage, and Medicaid
managed care lines of business. In Q4 2025, the Claims Operations division
processed 8.4 million claims totaling $3.8 billion in billed charges,
adjudicating $2.1 billion in allowed amounts and paying $1.74 billion
to providers.

Our overall auto-adjudication rate reached 78.2% (up from 71.4% in Q3),
driven by the ClaimsIQ rules engine upgrades led by VP of Claims Technology
Priya Patel. The Medical Loss Ratio (MLR) for Commercial was 82.1%,
Medicare Advantage was 86.4%, and Medicaid was 89.7% — all within target
ranges. CEO Robert Marsh has set a 2026 goal of reaching 85% auto-adjudication
while reducing the average cost per claim from $4.82 to $3.50.

SECTION 2: CLAIMS ADJUDICATION PROCESS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STEP 1 — CLAIMS INTAKE & FRONT-END PROCESSING
  Intake channels:
    EDI (837P/837I): 82% of total volume
    Clearinghouses: Availity (45%), Change Healthcare (30%), Trizetto (7%)
    Paper claims (OCR scanned): 14%
    Provider portal direct entry: 4%

  Front-end edits reject approximately 6.8% of incoming claims for:
    - Missing/invalid member ID (2.1%)
    - Missing/invalid provider NPI (1.4%)
    - Invalid CPT/HCPCS/ICD-10 codes (1.8%)
    - Duplicate claim detection (1.5%)

  Rejected claims are returned to providers with ANSI 277 status
  notifications within 24 hours.

STEP 2 — ELIGIBILITY & BENEFITS VERIFICATION
  Real-time 270/271 eligibility transactions: 1.2M per month
  Member eligibility match rate: 96.8% (auto-matched)
  Remaining 3.2% require manual research by enrollment team

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: Claims examiner veteran Linda Park (22 years at Meridian)
  has built an informal "retroactive eligibility" guide for Medicaid members
  whose coverage activates retroactively. The standard system denies these
  claims, but Linda knows which state-specific codes to override and has
  trained her team leads informally. This affects approximately 800 claims
  per month. If Linda retires, this knowledge could result in $2.4M in
  annual incorrect denials and provider abrasion.

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: For Medicare Advantage dual-eligible members (Medicare +
  Medicaid), the coordination of benefits between Medicare primary and
  Medicaid secondary often creates incorrect member liability calculations.
  Supervisor Tony Reeves developed an Excel-based "dual-eligible calculator"
  that the Boise team uses to manually verify these claims. This tool
  isn't integrated into ClaimsIQ. Tony estimates it prevents approximately
  $180K in monthly overpayments.

STEP 3 — PROVIDER VALIDATION & CONTRACT MATCHING
  In-network vs Out-of-network determination:
    - 89% of claims are from in-network providers
    - 8% out-of-network (No Surprises Act protections apply)
    - 3% out-of-area emergency (auto-pay at in-network rate)

  Fee schedule application:
    - Commercial PPO: Negotiated fee schedules per provider group
    - Commercial HMO: Capitated for PCP, FFS for specialists
    - Medicare Advantage: CMS Medicare fee schedule + supplemental
    - Medicaid: State-published fee schedule with MCO adjustments

  EXCEPTION: Provider group Northwest Medical Associates (142 providers,
  $48M annual spend) has a unique "carve-out" arrangement for orthopedic
  implant costs. The contract specifies that implant costs above $8,000
  per case are paid at invoice cost + 10% instead of the standard DRG
  rate. ClaimsIQ cannot handle this rule automatically — the implant
  claims team manually prices approximately 45 claims per month.
  Angela Torres has escalated this for system enhancement in Q1 2026.

  EXCEPTION: Emergency department claims from out-of-network providers
  must be processed under the No Surprises Act (NSA) Qualifying Payment
  Amount (QPA) methodology. The QPA calculation requires median contracted
  rates from our own data — but for rare specialties (pediatric neurosurgery,
  burn care), we have fewer than 5 contracted providers, making the median
  statistically unreliable. Steven Park's team uses CMS-provided reference
  data as a supplement, but this creates provider disputes in ~15% of NSA
  cases.

STEP 4 — CLINICAL REVIEW & MEDICAL NECESSITY
  Claims routed to clinical review:
    - Inpatient admissions (100% reviewed for medical necessity)
    - High-cost outpatient procedures > $5,000
    - Claims flagged by ClaimsIQ clinical rules (e.g., experimental
      procedure codes, off-label drug combinations)
    - Prior authorization mismatches

  Medical policy maintained by Dr. Sharma's team:
    - 842 medical policies active
    - Policy updates: 34 updated in Q4 (following MCG/InterQual updates)
    - Average review turnaround: 2.8 days (target: 2 days)

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: Dr. Sharma's team has found that MCG criteria for
  inpatient psychiatric admissions don't adequately capture the complexity
  of co-occurring substance use disorder cases. The clinical team has
  developed an informal "dual-diagnosis supplement" of 14 additional
  criteria points that they apply alongside MCG. This has reduced
  inappropriate denials by 23% for behavioral health cases. However,
  it's not formalized as official medical policy, creating inconsistency
  between reviewers.

  Prior Authorization compliance:
    - 94% of PA-required services had valid PA on file
    - 4% had PA but with date/quantity mismatches
    - 2% had no PA — reviewed on a case-by-case basis
    - Retrospective PA approval rate: 68% (for emergency situations)

STEP 5 — ADJUDICATION & PAYMENT
  Auto-adjudication rate: 78.2% (Q4 2025)
    Commercial: 82.4% | Medicare Advantage: 74.1% | Medicaid: 71.8%

  Manual examiner touch reasons:
    - COB / Third-party liability (24%)
    - Complex clinical review (18%)
    - Provider contract exceptions (16%)
    - Member cost-share calculation errors (14%)
    - Retroactive enrollment changes (12%)
    - System edit overrides requiring human review (16%)

  Payment cycles:
    - Electronic (EFT/ACH): 88% of payments, avg 12 days from receipt
    - Paper checks: 12%, avg 21 days from receipt
    - Clean claim prompt payment: 97.4% paid within state-mandated
      timeframes (30 days electronic, 45 days paper)

STEP 6 — PROVIDER DISPUTES & APPEALS
  Provider disputes received: 12,400 in Q4
  Resolution rate within 30 days: 84%
  Overturn rate: 22% (provider wins)

  Top dispute reasons:
    - Fee schedule / reimbursement rate (34%)
    - Bundling / unbundling edits (22%)
    - Medical necessity denials (18%)
    - Timely filing denials (14%)
    - Coordination of benefits (12%)

  TRIBAL KNOWLEDGE: Provider Relations specialist Maria Gonzalez has

  billing manager directly before a dispute is formally filed, resolving

  costs but is entirely dependent on Maria's personal relationships.

STEP 7 — MEMBER APPEALS & GRIEVANCES
  Member appeals filed: 3,200 in Q4

  External review overturn rate: 12%

  CMS Compliance (Medicare Advantage):

    - CTM (Complaint Tracking Module) cases: 142 (↓18% vs Q3)

INTEGRATION CHALLENGES:
  - Facets-to-ClaimsIQ handoff creates a 4-second latency per claim
    during peak hours (9am-1pm ET), causing batch processing delays
    of up to 2 hours for the afternoon payment cycle.
  - Provider directory sync between Facets and Availity portal has
    a 48-hour lag, leading to member-facing provider search showing
    stale network status (est. 200 member complaints/month).
  - Encounter data submission to CMS via QNXT has a 3.2% rejection
    rate due to data mapping issues between Facets and QNXT schemas.
    Lisa Ramirez's team manually corrects these before resubmission,
    consuming 120 staff-hours per month.
  - Apixio NLP accuracy for behavioral health records is only 72%
    (vs 91% for medical/surgical), requiring more manual review.
